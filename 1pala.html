Pala - The Land of Rubber Plantations

Located in Kottayam District, Kerala

About Pala

Pala is a beautiful town situated on the banks of the Meenachil River in Kerala, India. Known for its lush greenery, rich cultural heritage, and educational institutions, Pala is a blend of tradition and modernity.

Geography & Climate

Pala experiences a tropical monsoon climate, with heavy rainfall during the monsoon season. The town is surrounded by hills, rubber plantations, and rivers, making it a scenic location.

Key Attractions

  • St. Thomas Cathedral: One of the oldest churches in Kerala, known for its beautiful architecture.
  • Meenachil River: A serene river flowing through Pala, perfect for sightseeing.
  • St. Alphonsa Shrine, Bharananganam: A famous pilgrimage center dedicated to St. Alphonsa.
  • Illickan Mountain: A great trekking destination near Pala.

Economy

Pala is a major hub for rubber production in Kerala. Agriculture, trade, and education also contribute significantly to the economy.

Transportation

Pala is well-connected by road to major cities like Kottayam, Ernakulam, and Thiruvananthapuram. The nearest railway station is Kottayam, and the nearest airport is Cochin International Airport.
